Alexander III of Macedon was born in Pella in 356 BC, the son of King Philip II and the Epirote princess Olympias. From his earliest years he was set apart — tutored by the philosopher Aristotle, he developed a voracious intellect alongside a warrior's ambition.

At sixteen he crushed a Thracian uprising while his father was away. At eighteen he commanded the decisive cavalry charge at Chaeronea that ended Greek independence. At twenty, following his father's assassination, he seized the Macedonian throne — and never stopped moving.

In the thirteen years of his reign, Alexander never lost a battle.He shattered the Persian Empire, liberated Egypt, crossed the Hindu Kush, and pushed to the banks of the Beas River in modern-day India before his exhausted army finally refused to go further.
